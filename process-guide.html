How to Use This Guide

Process Overview
About This Guide

A process is only useful if you actually use it.

This guide is a repeatable production workflow, not a rulebook. It is built for junior designers, new hires, and freelancers who need to understand how a static ad moves from messy intake to clean delivery.

Some steps compress on tight deadlines. Some expand when the client is new, the offer is sensitive, or the ad must pass heavy platform review. The order still matters: each step feeds the next.

The goal is simple: reduce guesswork, make creative choices easier to defend, and build ads that look intentional instead of assembled at the last minute.

Step 01

Creative Brief Intake
Step 01

Creative Brief Intake

Also called: Client onboarding, brief review. Output: an approved creative brief.

The brief turns client context into a production target. Before opening Photoshop, capture the goal, audience, offer, deliverables, platform specs, deadline, brand constraints, and required claims.

Collect From Client
Social profiles and active ad accounts
Website URL and current landing page
Brand guidelines, logos, fonts, and product imagery
Past ads, best-performing content, and anything legally required

Common pitfall: accepting a thin brief. If the brief cannot explain the audience, the offer, and the ad format, the first design round becomes discovery by revision.

Creative Brief / Static Ad Campaign

Goal
Drive qualified leads for a single offer using 3 static ad variants.
Audience
Primary buyers, awareness stage, emotional triggers, objections.
Specs
Meta feed static ads, 4:5 ratio, mobile-first, logo visible.
Deadline
Concept approval by Friday. Final PNG exports after compliance pass.

Step 02

Visual Research & Moodboarding
Step 02

Visual Research & Moodboarding

Also called: Art direction research. When bundled with rationale: creative direction deck.

A moodboard creates shared visual language before production. It lets the designer compare references for lighting, composition, typography, color, and ad format without committing to a layout too early.

Tool of Choice

FigJam is ideal because references, notes, quick sketches, and strategy comments can live on the same board.

This step can be compressed or skipped on very small tasks under one hour, but it should stay active for new clients, new campaigns, or unfamiliar visual categories.

Reference board: use outside inspiration to identify patterns, not to copy finished ads.
Moodboard: photography, type, layouts, and color notes in one space.

Step 03

Creative Strategy
Step 03

Creative Strategy & Brief Expansion

Also called: Concept development. Output: creative brief expansion or concept doc.

This is where the rough brief becomes usable direction. The goal is to catch misalignment before a single pixel is placed.

Restate the objective in plain production language.
Define creative territories, hook options, and emotional angles.
Document typography, color, photo treatment, and layout direction.
Flag anything that requires client approval before production.

This step is the expansion of the moodboard. Anything useful found during research should land here as a decision, rationale, or constraint.

Step 05

AI Tools Overview
Step 05

Rapid Ideation with AI

AI outputs are reference only, never submission-ready. The value is speed and exploration: angles, layout scaffolds, headline structure, and fast concept pressure testing.

Tool Best For Pros Cons
Claude Brief analysis, reasoning, HTML templates Strong reasoning, reads and creates long docs, good at structured artifacts No native image generation, conversation limits at peak times
Gemini Research and image-generation starting points High limits, Google integration, audio and multimodal inputs Context can drift in long sessions
ChatGPT Reasoning and high-quality image generation Strong reasoning, strong image generation, good interactive iteration Upload and image-generation limits depend on plan
Muse Spark Simple general use with image and reasoning All-in-one, approachable, useful for quick drafts No standout specialty compared with dedicated tools

Agentic workflows exist, but they are outside the scope of this guide. This section focuses on practical static-ad production support.

Step 07

Legal & Compliance Review

Compliance review protects the client, the account, and the campaign. It is faster to flag a risky claim before launch than to repair a rejected ad later.

Review Checklist
General advertising: no misleading claims, fake urgency, or unsupported promises.
Apparel and product: accurate materials, sizing, pricing, and availability.
Medical or regulated categories: confirm claims and platform restrictions.
Platform policies: Meta, TikTok, Google Ads, and any client-specific restrictions.
Pro tip: A five-minute flag with the account manager beats a rejected ad after launch.

Step 08

Compositing
Step 08

Compositing — Creative Execution

Also called: Art direction and production.

Set up canvas at the correct platform dimensions.
Bring in comps and guides from the approved brief expansion.
Build background, product, typography, branding, and CTA in layer groups.
Fine-tune spacing, hierarchy, contrast, legibility, and safe-zone placement.
Export final flats and save the master PSD.

Step 09

A/B Variation Production
Step 09

A/B Variation Production

Variation work tests one meaningful variable at a time. Two variants is the minimum, three to five is standard, and ten or more usually creates noise unless there is a clear testing plan.

Var 1: Control - original product hook and direct headline.
Var 2: Headline and layout test using same core product direction.
Var 3: Visual balance test with adjusted emphasis and hierarchy.
Naming Convention

[Client]_[Campaign]_[AdName]_[Hook]_[Variable]_[Variant#].png

This Process Is Modular.

On a tight deadline, steps 2-4 can be condensed. On a new client, you may spend extra time on steps 4 and 7. The order matters because each step feeds the next. Skip a step at your own risk, but know the skipped step usually returns as a revision or a problem later.
